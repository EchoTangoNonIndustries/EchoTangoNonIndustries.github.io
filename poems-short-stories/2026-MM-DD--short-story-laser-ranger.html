A Laser Ranger's Journey to the White Spire

The Beginning of the Start After the End

Why do I feel hung-over? I haven't drunk alcohol since uni. I don't have any medications or addictions, so I can't be in withdrawal. Whatever - it's my days off, and I'm going camping. I'll take some painkillers and drink some coffee while I pack my bag. It looks like the moon's out - I must have woken up in the middle of the night. I'll throw open the curtains, so I can --

OK, this is different. The whole city's in a blackout. Good thing the moon is --
A massive pillar reaching down from the sky?
...
The sky's messed up too - it's like a hazy mist, but... Everything looks washed out, like an old TV with the color half-way burned out to black-and-white.

Shit, this isn't just a blackout either - there's a couple cars and shops on fire, and I can see smashed windows.
...
Well, somehow I slept through a city-wide riot. ...And someone built a magical space-elevator. Like, the engineer's barely started figuring out nanotubes for short wires and strings - kilometers of the stuff is out of the question. Plus it'd be a silver-gray, not this brilliant white. Even the moon doesn't look this bright.

Okey-dokey. The world appears to be fucked, and this white spire seems like an obvious destination. If it's filled with rich assholes and their security guards and killbots, I'll fight my way in or die trying. Slowly starving to death out here doesn't seem like a great way to go out. Especially without anyone to talk to, or even just a box of movies, music and porn.

Time to figure out what supplies I've got. My pistol's in its holster beside the bed, and I've got spare batteries. I've got my backpack I use for going to work, or day-hikes on my days off. Clear out the bathroom - toothbrush, painkillers, anti-diarrheal and the rest... These adrenaline pens I kept for when my cousin Zez visited might be useful too. Good thing I don't have any prescriptions - Zez had a rough time when they forgot their antidepressants at home.

I was going to go camping next week with my ex and my cousin, so I've got a bunch of dried food ready to pack. Nuts and raisins, the fish jerky I wanted to try, instant noodles, plus a couple military rations - pork stew with biscuits, and chicken sausages with buckwheat and cranberry salad - sounds nice! I can usually fit my summer sleeping bag in here, but Zez always had the tent. Did I ever buy that emergency heat-tarp-tent thing...?

OK, let's check this shelf - folding mini-stove, water filter, emergency radio, flame-lighters, zap-lighters... Right. I got started making a waterproof cloak-tarp. I still have power and the supplies are still sitting in this box - Fuck, I still need water. I've got two liter-bottles, and three handies - that should be enough for a day and a half easily - it's pretty temperate in this...blackened void of a land. Not sure how the hell that works - without the sun, we should be freezing.

Okey-dokey, this is already bigger than I can fit in my pack, and I don't have any wheels either. I better check on the street - my truck better still be there. The power's out in the building, but this flashlight's good enough. Down three flights of stairs, and out the door...
...
Half good news half bad news. The good news is my truck is still parked here. The bad news is some bastards smashed the windows and stripped it for parts. Not sure why they didn't steal the thing first - Maybe some kids smashed it when things first went to shit, and other goons stripped it later.

That complicates things somewhat. There's a few other smashed cars, motorcycles, that trashed van a homeless person was sleeping in... You're kidding me - the only thing nobody stole or wrecked - the teenager's motor-scooter they used to get to school and deliver pizzas. I guess it was too boring-looking and small to be noticed. The little cargo trailer behind it's still attached too.

Service With A Circuit

The door chimes weakly as I walk through the door, and the lights flicker from dim to mediocre. A scuffed-up robot stands behind the desk at the back of the shop.

"Good evening madam! My stock is limited - the delivery van hasn't shown up in 728 days. I've got canned and dried foods, personal hygiene items, and some camp-craft basics. Take a look around!"

"I'll grab this oilskin cloak, some dried food, and these mini soap tablets. Plus this tire-patch kit for my scooter outside. Do you have any high-power batteries, or can you charge these ones? I was in a firefight with ghouls." I say, tapping the bandolier at my chest.

"I have several behind the counter; You'll be charged for the energy plus any difference in wear and tear. How will madam be paying today?"

"Cash card?"

"Excellent choice madam - the connection to credit-card companies has been non-functional for 696 days. May I suggest some spare tampons? You never know, especially these days!"

"No thanks - I've got a squirmle, I'm single, and I've got more important things to think about than potential future children. If I meet anyone people who aren't trying to kill me, they'd better be men, or have their own supplies.
...
Hell, you've put me in a good mood. Do you have any squirmle egg-pills? I can hand them out to any women I happen to meet."

"Luckily they come in twelve-packs for young women in university sororities. I'll add one to your bill!"

And with that, I was out and back on the road. Riding from light to light, dodging whatever shadows I could. The number of lamp-posts that were out of service was about as bad here as I'd already ridden past, and about as bad as what I could see ahead. Which is to say, pretty spotty coverage, with haze and black peppering my way to the white spire.
